⬦ How to determine whether two terms (e.g. Term A and Term B) are associated or not?
  • NA: the number of genes annotated in Term A.
  • NB: the number of genes annotated in Term B.
  • NAB: the number of genes annotated in both Term A & Term B.
  • NY=6611: the number of ORFs (open reading frames) in the yeast genome.
  • Term A and Term B are regarded as ASSOCIATED if their member genes have significant overlap (i.e. NAB is big enough).
  • The p-value of significant overlap is calculated using the hypergeometric test as follows.
  • The p-value is then corrected by the FDR (false discovery rate) to represent the true alpha level in the multiple hypotheses testing.
  • Finally, Term A and Term B are said to be ASSOCIATED with each other if the corrected p-value <= 0.01.
